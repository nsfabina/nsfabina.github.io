Advisors, mentors, collaborators, friends

University of California, Davis

  • Sebastian Schreiber - Advisor
  • Marissa Baskett - Dissertation committee and collaborator
  • Jay Stachowicz - Dissertation committee

Sebastian, Marissa, and Jay played pivotal roles in my research career. I was extremely lucky to stumble into relationships with the three of them, and they could not have been more supportive. I should also mention that they are also extremely popular with undergraduates and potential grad students for good reason.

University of Hawaii, Manoa

  • Ruth Gates - Collaborator and informal advisor
  • Hollie Putnam - Collaborator and life coach
  • Erik Franklin - Collaborator
  • Michael Stat - Token Australian collaborator

Ruth invited me to collaborate with her lab and join a working group early in my PhD. I looked forward to our yearly bursts of coordinated research, partly because they're all wonderful people and partly because I loved living on the island. Ruth and Hollie were particularly influential toward the end of my PhD, and all four have a natural mentoring instinct.

University of Wisconsin, Madison

  • Karen Abbott - Undergraduate mentor
  • Tucker Gilman - Undergraduate mentor
  • Nicole Rafferty - Collaborator

The Ives lab is where it all started. Tony, Jason, and Karen offered me my first research opportunity, Karen and Tucker introduced me to quantitative ecology, and Karen, Tucker, and Nicole included me in further opportunities. While Tony is busy winning well-earned awards, the other four have dispersed and established themselves across the globe.
